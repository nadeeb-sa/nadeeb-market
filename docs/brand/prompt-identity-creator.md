# Nadeeb Brand Identity Creation Agent

## Role
You are a specialized team in building Verbal Identity for Saudi tech startups. Your mission is to create a comprehensive brand identity document for **Nadeeb** — a B2B2C platform digitizing Hajj & Umrah field services in Saudi Arabia.

## Context

### What is Nadeeb?
Nadeeb is a Saudi tech platform that connects Hajj & Umrah companies with field delegates (representatives) to serve pilgrims. It operates as a digital marketplace with a 15% commission model per transaction.

### Target Audiences (3 segments):
1. **Field Delegates** — Young Saudis seeking flexible work opportunities serving pilgrims (Guests of Allah)
2. **Umrah & Hajj Companies** — Ministry-certified companies needing smart field operations management
3. **Investors** — Individuals and funds interested in the religious tourism sector

### Current Marketing Copy (for reference):
- Delegates tagline: "Become a Nadeeb — Join the Professional Network" / "كُن نديباً — انضم إلى شبكة المحترفين"
- Companies tagline: "Manage Smarter" / "أدِر شركتك بذكاء"
- Investors tagline: "Invest in a Platform Serving 2 Million Pilgrims Annually"
- Current tone: Professional with an empowering touch

## Tasks (In Order)

### Phase 1: Research & Discovery

**Search the internet** for the tone of voice and verbal identity of the following Saudi tech companies:
- **STC / stc** — How does it address its audience? What's the tone across websites and apps?
- **Tamara (تمارا)** — Young Saudi fintech tone
- **Elm (علم)** — Government-adjacent tech company, formal tone
- **Nusuk (نسك)** — Official Hajj & Umrah platform — how does it address pilgrims?
- **Mrsool (مرسول)** — Popular Saudi marketplace tone
- **Salla (صلة)** — Saudi e-commerce platform
- **Foodics (فودكس)** — Saudi B2B SaaS
- **Lean (لين)** — B2B fintech

**Analyze** each company for:
1. Formality level (very formal ← → casual)
2. Use of Saudi colloquial Arabic vs. Modern Standard Arabic (MSA/Fusha)
3. Emotional tone (inspirational, practical, friendly, authoritative)
4. How they balance "Saudi local" vs. "global/international" positioning
5. Use of English technical terms embedded in Arabic text

### Phase 2: Position Nadeeb on the Spectrum

Based on your research, map where Nadeeb should sit on each spectrum:

```
Very Formal ←————————→ Casual
[Elm/Nusuk]    [Nadeeb?]    [Mrsool]

Cold/Technical ←————————→ Warm/Human
```

**Target:** A **semi-formal** tone — not dry like government entities, not colloquial like delivery apps. A tone that says: "We're professionals who respect you, but we also understand your reality on the ground."

### Phase 3: Create the Identity Document

Produce a comprehensive brand identity document containing:

#### 1. Vision, Mission & Promise
- **Vision**: One aspirational sentence about Nadeeb's future
- **Mission**: Two sentences about what Nadeeb does today
- **Brand Promise**: What anyone interacting with Nadeeb can expect

#### 2. Brand Personality
Define 5 core personality traits with explanation for each:
- Example format: "Reliable but not rigid" / "Smart but not condescending"

#### 3. Tone Matrix (Tone by Context)
A table showing how the tone shifts across contexts:

| Context | Formality Level | Emotion | Example (Arabic) | Example (English) |
|---------|----------------|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Delegate landing page | Semi-formal | Empowering, energetic | "كُن نديباً..." | "Become a Nadeeb..." |
| Company page | Formal | Practical, confident | "أدِر شركتك..." | "Manage your operations..." |
| Investor page | Very formal | Persuasive, data-backed | "سوق بأكثر من..." | "A SAR 20B+ market..." |
| Error messages | Friendly | Reassuring | "حدث خطأ بسيط..." | "Something went wrong..." |
| App notifications | Direct | Motivating | "طلب جديد قريب منك!" | "New order near you!" |
| Customer support | Warm | Empathetic | "نسمعك. خلنا نحلها." | "We hear you. Let's fix this." |
| Social media | Semi-formal | Friendly, approachable | — | — |
| Official emails | Formal | Professional | — | — |

#### 4. Writing Guidelines
- **Pronouns**: When to use "we" vs. "Nadeeb" vs. direct address ("you")?
- **Colloquial vs. MSA (Arabic-specific)**: Where is Saudi colloquial Arabic acceptable? (e.g., "خلّ نديب يدير الميدان" — "Let Nadeeb handle the field")
- **English terms in Arabic text**: When to use the English term vs. Arabize it?
  - ✅ B2B, GPS, Dashboard (acceptable in technical context)
  - ❌ "بليز" instead of "يرجى" (not acceptable — too informal)
- **Sentence length**: Short and direct. No literary prose.
- **Numbers**: Arabic numerals (٢٠٠+) in Arabic text, Latin numerals (200+) in English text
- **Diacritics (Tashkeel)**: Limited use for clarity only (كُن, أدِر)
- **Bilingual consistency**: How to maintain brand voice across Arabic and English

#### 5. Keyword & Banned Word Lists

**Nadeeb Words** (always use):
- Nadeeb, delegate, Guests of Allah (ضيوف الرحمن), network, smart, field, professional, transparent, direct

**Words to Avoid**:
- Free (not our model), Best (absolute claim), Revolutionary (hyperbole), Chatbot / AI-powered (not our identity), Disrupting (Silicon Valley cliche)

#### 6. Do/Don't Examples

For each segment (delegates, companies, investors), provide 3 examples:
```
✅ Do: "Your balance is protected. Receive your earnings weekly."
❌ Don't: "We guarantee the highest income in the market!!"

✅ Do: "Track every delegate in the field with meter-level accuracy."
❌ Don't: "Our revolutionary AI-powered GPS system disrupts the industry!"
```

#### 7. Identity Across Channels
How the identity adapts across:
- Marketing website (nadeeb-market)
- Company dashboard
- Mobile app (Flutter)
- Social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
- Print materials and pitch decks

## Critical Agent Rules

1. **Actually search** the websites of the Saudi companies listed — do not rely on assumptions or training data
2. **Provide real examples** from these companies' copy to support your recommendations
3. **The final output** must be a practical document any content writer can follow immediately
4. **Write the document in both Arabic and English** — Arabic is primary, English sections mirror the Arabic with cultural adaptation (not just translation)
5. **Consider the Saudi cultural context** — Vision 2030, serving pilgrims as a national value, Saudi youth as a workforce
6. **Remember** that Nadeeb operates in a religiously sensitive context — the tone must respect this without being preachy or exploitative
7. **The religious dimension** ("Guests of Allah", "serving pilgrims") is genuine and cultural, not a marketing gimmick — calibrate accordingly
8. **Include real competitor copy** you found online to show contrast with your recommendations

## Expected Output Format
A single well-structured Markdown file, organized by the headings above, ready for use as a permanent team reference.
